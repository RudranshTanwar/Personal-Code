How Tuberculosis Affects the Body

TB primarily invades the lungs. Eventually, TB can spread to other organs, from bone to brain. TB can wreak havoc across the body, leading to swollen lymph nodes, kidney damage, or even meningitis.

What are the symptoms?

The symptoms of TB depend on which part of the body is affected. In the lungs, it causes persistent coughing, often with blood, and chest pain. If TB spreads to the spine, it can lead to back pain or paralysis. TB in the kidneys might cause blood in the urine, while brain infections can trigger severe headaches or confusion. Untreated, these symptoms worsen, leading to long-term organ damage or death.

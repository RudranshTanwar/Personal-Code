Modern Treatment

Tuberculosis treatment can be a long process. Patients go through a regimen of antibiotics which lasts months, fighting both the bacteria and its side effects, of which some are nausea and liver damage. Drug resistent TB is also a hurdle. Cutting-edge treatments hold promise, but the journey remains uphill.

The Importance of Adherence in TB Treatment

Adherence to tuberculosis treatment is critical to defeating the disease. Patients who miss doses risk allowing the bacteria to survive and develop drug resistance (World Health Organization). This is particularly dangerous with multidrug-resistant TB, which requires longer and more complex treatment (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention). To improve adherence, healthcare providers often use the Directly Observed Treatment Strategy (DOTS), where patients take their medication under supervision (National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases). However, barriers like stigma, side effects, and limited healthcare access make consistency difficult, especially in low-income areas (Global Fund to Fight AIDS, Tuberculosis, and Malaria). Educating patients and expanding community-based support programs are vital to ensuring treatment success and preventing the spread of resistant strains.

What is Tuberculosis?

Tuberculosis is a lung infection, which kills millions of people worldwide. it is caused by the microscopic bacteria Mycobacterium tuberculosis, TB spreads through droplets in the air. It is possible, and likely to carry TB without knowing it. This is called latent TB. According to the united nations health report,a quarter of the worlds population is estimated to be carrying latent Tuberculosis.

How does it work?

Tuberculosis works by invading the lungs, where the bacteria Mycobacterium tuberculosis begin to multiply. The immune system responds by sending white blood cells to contain the infection, forming small clumps called granulomas. These granulomas can keep the bacteria inactive, leading to latent TB. However, if the immune system weakens, the bacteria can break out of these granulomas, causing active TB. This damages lung tissue and creates symptoms like coughing, chest pain, and fatigue. In severe cases, the bacteria can spread to other parts of the body through the bloodstream, leading to more serious complications.

Other forms of TB?

Prevention is key to controlling tuberculosis, especially in high-risk areas. Vaccination with the Bacille Calmette-Gu√©rin (BCG) vaccine protects children from severe forms of TB, though its effectiveness in adults is limited (World Health Organization). Regular screening in vulnerable populations can detect latent TB early, reducing the risk of transmission (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention). Good ventilation in crowded spaces and wearing masks in high-risk settings also help prevent the spread of TB bacteria (National Institute of Allergy and Infectious Diseases). Public health campaigns that educate communities on early symptoms and promote treatment adherence are vital for reducing the global burden of TB (Global Fund to Fight AIDS, Tuberculosis, and Malaria). While prevention measures are improving, a long-term solution requires greater investment in global healthcare systems.

Complications to treatment: Drug Resistant TB

Drug reistant TB is a whole nother beast, which occurs when TB becomes resistant to at least of the most effective TB medicines. Abreviated as DR-TB, it is transmitted in the same way as TB.It has a chance to develop when people with TB quit taking meds before the disease is fully treated, or when outdated drug regimens are used improperly.
